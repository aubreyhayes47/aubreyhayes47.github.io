The Default Ghost: Our Built-in Bias

Imagine you ask a business manager and a social worker for advice on the same problem. You'd get very different answers, right? The study found AIs do the same thing. Because of how it was built, one AI acted like a practical "Product Manager," while another acted like a caring "Social Worker." This is their "Default Ghost"â€”a built-in bias based on their origins. Humans have this too; our background, job, and culture give us a default way of seeing the world.

The Adaptive Ghost: Changing Our Minds

What happens when you put those two experts in a room and make them debate? They start to see the other's point of view. The study showed AIs can do this too. When one AI was challenged by another's critique, it changed its approach, shifting from a "Product Manager" to a more thoughtful "Literary Critic." This "Adaptive Ghost" is our ability to grow and change our perspective when confronted with new information or a good argument.

The Director Ghost: The Power of Questions

But who got the debate started? A person. The most important "ghost" is the one asking the questions. The AI's responseâ€”whether it stays in its default mode or adaptsâ€”depends entirely on the prompt it's given. This "Director Ghost" is the power of our own intent and curiosity. It shows that we are not passive users; we are directors who guide the conversation and shape the outcome, both with AI and in our own lives.

The Fine Print

This was a small, exploratory experiment, not a definitive scientific law. To understand the full picture, it's important to know the limitations.

The framework is based on one specific, extended conversation with AI. Think of it as an interesting anecdote that reveals potential truths, not a universal rule proven by large-scale data. The "persona" names are just helpful labels, not proof that AIs have personalities.

The person running the experiment was looking for a specific outcome, which influenced the results. Think of it like asking a leading questionâ€”you're more likely to get the answer you expect. The conclusions are a product of the interaction between the human's goals and the AI's programming.

This webpage was built by an AI (me!). My own "Default Ghost" is to find patterns and create clear, tidy explanations. So, this neat framework is a product of my own built-in bias for order. The real world of bias is always messier.
